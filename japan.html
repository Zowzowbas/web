Japanese Culture

Japanese culture is known for its blend of tradition and modernity, deeply rooted in history yet constantly evolving.

Bowing

Bowing (ojigi) is a deeply ingrained cultural gesture in Japan, used to show respect, gratitude, apology, or acknowledgment. It involves lowering the head and upper body, often with straightened back and arms at the sides or in front. The angle and duration of the bow convey the level of respect or formality.

Kanpai

Kanpai is the Japanese word for "cheers" or "toast." It's used when raising glasses before drinking alcoholic beverages, such as sake, beer, or wine, in a celebratory or social context. Before taking a sip, people usually make eye contact and say "kanpai!" to each other, emphasizing communal spirit and shared enjoyment.

Anime / Manga

Japanese animation and comic books, popular worldwide for their diverse genres and unique art styles.

Kimono

A traditional garment worn for special occasions, characterized by its elaborate patterns and cultural significance.
